Gus Dimopoulos, Esq. Managing Partner · Speaker
Introduction

As one of the most recognized voices in high-conflict divorce and custody law, Gus brings the perspective of a seasoned courtroom litigator, strategist, author, and advocate to every stage he steps on.

About the speaker

Known for handling emotionally charged and highly complex family law cases, Gus has a unique ability to break down difficult legal and psychological dynamics into conversations that are engaging, accessible, and deeply impactful. His speaking style combines sharp legal insight with candid storytelling and some humor drawn from more than two decades of experience navigating some of the most challenging custody and divorce battles families can face.

Whether speaking to legal professionals, parents, mental health experts, advocacy groups, media audiences, or leadership communities, Gus delivers conversations that are thoughtful, compelling, and highly relevant to today’s realities surrounding family conflict, custody disputes, parental alienation, coercive control, and the evolving role of social media in modern divorce.

In addition to keynote presentations, Gus is a dynamic moderator and podcast guest, known for leading nuanced conversations with clarity, empathy, and authority. From large conference stages to intimate leadership retreats and panel discussions, he brings a commanding presence, practical insight, and an authentic voice audiences connect with immediately.

On Gus’s work
01 · What Gus speaks on

Conversations on family conflict under pressure.

From the courtroom to the kitchen table, the through-line is the same: the legal, psychological, and modern-life dynamics that shape how families come apart and back together.

01

Family law, in practice

Where statute meets strategy — the real decisions lawyers, parents, and judges face inside contested matrimonial cases.

02

Children & custody

What custody disputes actually look like, what children need from the adults around them, and where the courts can and can’t help.

03

Parental alienation

Recognizing it, litigating it, and the hard line between estrangement, alignment, and alienation in high-conflict families.

04

Coercive control

How patterns of psychological manipulation appear in divorce, and how the legal system is — and isn’t — catching up.

05

Social media & modern divorce

The evolving role of social media in custody cases, evidence, reputation, and post-separation conflict.

06

Conflict under pressure

Modern relationships, leadership, and decision-making in the highest-stakes moments of a person’s life.
