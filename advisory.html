Premise

Most high-performing professionals have contingency plans for every risk that matters — market volatility, succession gaps, reputational exposure, leadership failure. There is one risk that rarely makes the list, and it is responsible for some of the most devastating personal, professional, and financial outcomes in high-performance environments: divorce, family instability, behavioral breakdown, and the slow, quiet erosion of the relationships that make everything else worth having.

About Gus

The advisor who has seen how these stories end.

Gus Dimopoulos has spent decades at the center of high-conflict divorce and family law in New York — advising executives, entrepreneurs, financiers, and ultra-high-net-worth families through some of the most consequential moments of their lives.

What he observed changed how he works. Long before a marriage breaks down publicly, there are patterns — behavioral, emotional, communicative — that quietly set the collapse in motion. Patterns he recognized, case after case.

Today, Gus uses that pattern recognition in a different way: helping high-performing professionals identify and interrupt those dynamics before they become crises.

This is not therapy. It is not corporate HR training. It is direct, confidential, strategic guidance informed by decades of watching personal and professional collapse unfold in real time.

The risk no one talks about in finance.
